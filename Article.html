PROJECT

During the Digital Media Sprintweek, we tried to find a way to create a decentralized network. We thought of making a "program", in which you can share text based files, that works via a Partial Mesh Network. This Nintendo DS was the starting point of our project. We remembered that the nintendo from our childhood already had some nice technology going on. Both PICTOCHAT and the DS Download Play are working with a Mesh Network.

Then we had our first presentation about Mesh Networking. In this presentation we discussed what Mesh Networking is and how it works. We also showed some examples everybody will recognize, that already use this Mesh Networking.

After all the research we did, we started sketching what the "program" would look like. While doing so we also discussed which options should be included. Like there should be a public and a personal library, and there should be an "add" button and a "remove" button, etc.

As you can see this whole site itself is build the same way as we want our program to look like. The only thing thats different is that the text on the public library is replaced with images and overlaying text. In the original version this will not be the case since it's a text based program.


We also wrote a Manifest in which the whole project is explained:

In today’s society, a lot of information is corrupted. It’s filtered and that is actually not your fault. But now we have a solution. The level of filtered information now depends on you and only you.

The more people and places you visit, the more information you gain. On the other hand, you will not be able to filter. You receive all the information from the people/places that you visit, you don’t have the ability to be selective in that area. So the only way you can influence your data input is to the places you go to, or deciding not to read the article. Your selectivity won’t be fed by influencers of the higher power. Maybe now you think, how will I be able to store all this information? You don’t. You are in charge of what you save, everything that you don’t save will disappear after 24 hours.

So in short, you have a public page which loads text files (PDF, word, text) from people you encounter on a daily basis. Then it’s up to you to add a few of these files to your personal library where it will be stored until you decide you’re done with it. This is the start of a completely distributed network in which hierarchy doesn’t exist anymore and in which not only all people are equal but are also appreciated.
